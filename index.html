Frequently Asked Questions

Flowers Dropping or Withering?

Drooping or wilting flowers may indicate overwatering or insufficient light. Adjust watering frequency and ensure proper sunlight exposure to revive them.

What makes flower leaves turn pale?

Pale flower leaves are often a sign of nutrient deficiency or inadequate sunlight. Consider fertilizing with a balanced plant food and relocating to a brighter spot for healthier foliage.

What leads to brown, dry leaves on flowers

Brown, dry leaves on flowers can result from underwatering, excessive sunlight, or nutrient imbalance. Check soil moisture, adjust watering, and provide proper light conditions to prevent further leaf damage.

How do I select the right soil for my flowers?

Choose soil specifically formulated for your flower type, ensuring it offers good drainage and adequate nutrients. Look for a well-balanced potting mix tailored to the needs of your particular flowers.

How do I change the pots?

When repotting flowers, gently remove them from their current pot, loosen the roots, and place them in a slightly larger container with fresh, well-draining soil. Water thoroughly after repotting to help them adjust.

What attracts gnats to my flowers?

Gnats are often attracted to moist soil and decaying organic matter in flower pots. To deter them, allow the soil to dry out between waterings, remove any dead plant material, and consider using sticky traps or natural repellents like neem oil.
